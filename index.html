Our Story

Four Generations,
One Sacred Mission

From the lanes of Kasur, Pakistan, to the heart of Bathinda, India — the Kataria family's century-long journey of Ayurvedic healing is a story of resilience, devotion, and unbroken service.

1st Generation · Founder
Baba Bahali Ram Kataria
Pre-Partition Era · Kasur, Punjab (now Pakistan)
The great patriarch of this lineage established the first dawakhana in Kasur with nothing but deep knowledge of herbs and an unshakeable desire to serve. With limited resources but limitless compassion, he treated countless patients, earning the trust and reverence of an entire community.
2nd Generation · Vaidya Khaki
Vaidya Kundan Lal Kataria
Post-Partition · Migration to India
Fondly known as "Vaidya Khaki Ji," he carried his father's torch through the upheaval of Partition, bringing the Dawakhana to Indian soil. His mastery of Ayurvedic formulations and his warm bedside manner attracted thousands of patients — raising the clinic's reputation to new heights.
3rd Generation · Current Head
Vaid Pardeep Kataria
Present Day · Bathinda, Punjab, India
Carrying forward the family's rich tradition, Vaid Pardeep Kataria Ji has expanded the reach of this Dawakhana to serve patients across India and internationally via phone and online consultations. His expertise in complex chronic conditions — especially kidney disease, diabetes, and skin disorders — has helped thousands regain their health.
4th Generation · Future of the Legacy
Dr. Oshin Kataria (BAMS)
Present Day · Bathinda, Punjab, India
The newest guardian of the Kataria legacy, Dr. Oshin Kataria brings formal BAMS education to a foundation of deep family knowledge. She blends classical Ayurvedic principles with modern diagnostic understanding, ensuring that the next century of this Dawakhana is built on both tradition and contemporary excellence.
